Codeshare

What is a codeshare agreement?

It is an agreement between two airlines in which one airline markets the other airline's flights using its own code in order to be able to offer more destination options.

How to recognize a codeshare flight

When you are selecting itineraries on copa.com, codeshare flights will be identified with a reference number next to the flight number. This number refers to a footnote indicating which airline will operate the flight.

In this example, flight CM5628 has an indicator identifying United Airlines as the operating airline on the LAX-HNL route. Since it is a codeshare, the flight number begins with the letters ‘CM,’ which is Copa Airlines’ marketing code. You will also be able to identify the operating airline on your electronic itinerary.

Itinerary changes

If you want to make a change in your itinerary please call the reservations center or stop by one of Copa Airlines' sales offices.

Any involuntary itinerary changes and/or cancellations during the day of the flight (0-48 hours before the departure of the flight), are handled by the operating airline.

Any involuntary itinerary changes and/or cancellations before the day of the flight (more than 48 hours before departure) are handled by the airline.

The responsible airline, in any case, will be in charge of contacting the affected passengers and make timely changes in the reservation.

Codeshare baggage policy

We recommend checking the interline baggage policy before purchasing a codeshare flight as the rules vary according to the routes in your itinerary.
